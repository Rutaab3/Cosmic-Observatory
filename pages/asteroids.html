Asteroids

Rocky remnants from the solar system's formation, ranging from pebbles to dwarf planets

What are Asteroids?

Asteroids are rocky objects that orbit the Sun, ranging in size from small pebbles to objects hundreds of kilometers across. They are remnants from the solar system's formation 4.6 billion years ago, representing the building blocks that never assembled into planets.

Most asteroids are found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, though they exist throughout the solar system. These ancient objects provide valuable insights into the early solar system and the processes that formed the planets.

1.1 million known asteroids Size: 1m to 940km Age: 4.6 billion years Mostly in asteroid belt

Types of Asteroids

Classification based on composition and spectral properties

C-type (Carbonaceous)

C-type asteroids are the most common, making up about 75% of known asteroids. They are dark, carbon-rich objects that likely contain water and organic compounds, making them particularly interesting for astrobiology.

Characteristics:
75% of asteroids Dark surface (3-9% reflectivity) Carbon-rich composition May contain water ice
Examples:

Ceres: The largest asteroid and only dwarf planet in the asteroid belt.

Pallas: Second-largest asteroid with an unusual tilted orbit.

Hygiea: Fourth-largest asteroid with a nearly spherical shape.

S-type (Silicaceous)

S-type asteroids are composed mainly of silicate minerals and nickel-iron, making them brighter than C-types. They represent about 17% of known asteroids and are more common in the inner asteroid belt.

Characteristics:
17% of asteroids Moderate reflectivity (10-22%) Silicate and metal composition Inner asteroid belt
Examples:

Vesta: Second-most massive asteroid with a differentiated interior.

Juno: One of the first asteroids discovered, highly elongated shape.

Hebe: Possible source of H chondrite meteorites on Earth.

M-type (Metallic)

M-type asteroids are composed primarily of nickel-iron metal and are thought to be the exposed cores of differentiated asteroids that were disrupted by collisions. They are relatively rare but highly reflective.

Characteristics:
8% of asteroids High reflectivity (10-18%) Nickel-iron composition Exposed planetary cores
Examples:

Psyche: Largest M-type asteroid, target of NASA mission.

Kleopatra: Dog-bone shaped asteroid with two moons.

Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs)

Near-Earth asteroids have orbits that bring them close to Earth's orbit. While most pose no threat, some are classified as Potentially Hazardous Asteroids (PHAs) and are closely monitored.

Categories:

Atira: Orbits entirely within Earth's orbit

Aten: Semi-major axis less than 1 AU

Apollo: Semi-major axis greater than 1 AU

Amor: Orbits between Earth and Mars

28,000+ known NEAs 2,300+ PHAs Continuous monitoring

The Asteroid Belt

The main concentration of asteroids between Mars and Jupiter

Structure and Formation

The asteroid belt is a torus-shaped region between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter, containing most of the solar system's known asteroids. Despite popular depictions, the belt is mostly empty space with asteroids separated by millions of kilometers.

Formation Theory:

The asteroid belt likely formed from material that was prevented from accreting into a planet by Jupiter's gravitational influence. Jupiter's gravity stirred up the region, causing high-velocity collisions that prevented planetary formation.

Kirkwood Gaps:

The belt contains gaps called Kirkwood gaps, regions where few asteroids are found due to orbital resonances with Jupiter. These resonances destabilize asteroid orbits, clearing out certain distances.

Distance: 2.2 - 3.2 AU Total mass: 4% of Moon 1.1-1.9 million asteroids >1km Ceres contains 25% of belt mass

Asteroid Exploration

Spacecraft missions to study asteroids up close

Past and Current Missions

Dawn Mission (2007-2018)

NASA's Dawn spacecraft visited both Vesta and Ceres, providing detailed maps and discovering water ice on Ceres and evidence of past volcanic activity on Vesta.

Visited Vesta & Ceres Ion propulsion
OSIRIS-REx (2016-2023)

NASA's sample return mission to asteroid Bennu, successfully collected samples and returned them to Earth in September 2023.

Sample return Bennu asteroid
Hayabusa2 (2014-2020)

JAXA's mission to asteroid Ryugu, successfully returned samples to Earth and deployed small rovers on the asteroid's surface.

Ryugu samples Surface rovers

Future Missions

Psyche Mission (2023-2029)

NASA mission to study the metallic asteroid Psyche, believed to be the exposed core of an early planet. Launch occurred in October 2023.

Metal asteroid Planetary core study
DART Mission Results

NASA's Double Asteroid Redirection Test successfully impacted Dimorphos in 2022, demonstrating planetary defense capabilities.

Planetary defense Successful impact
Lucy Mission (2021-2033)

NASA mission to study Jupiter's Trojan asteroids, primitive objects that share Jupiter's orbit and may hold clues to solar system formation.

Trojan asteroids 12-year mission
